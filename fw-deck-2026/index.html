The Problem

95% of India's salaried professionals cannot afford a financial advisor.
The free alternatives are lying to them.

"What do I actually have?"

HDFC salary account, SBI savings, Groww SIPs, forgotten PPF, dad's LIC policy — 8 to 15 accounts, no single view. You don't even know your own net worth.

"Where does my money go?"

You earned ₹80K this month. You can account for ₹50K. The other ₹30K? Your brain literally cannot track 200+ transactions across UPI, cards, and cash. No human can.

"How much can I actually invest?"

You started a ₹10K SIP, broke it in 3 months because it was too aggressive. You needed ₹6K — but nobody told you that number.

"Am I saving enough for my child's education?"

Your daughter starts college in 12 years. You have no idea if your current savings will cover it, or how much more you need each month.

"What should I do with my money?"

You have ₹28K surplus. Should it go to the emergency fund? Retirement? That car you want? In what proportion? Nobody can answer this — because nobody has your full picture.

And the "free" advice is a sales pitch

Bank RMs, insurance agents, MF distributors all earn commissions from what they recommend. Their incentive is to sell you products, not solve your life.

A human advisor solves all of these — for ₹1-2 lakh/year. 80M+ salaried Indians can't afford that.

Why Can't Incumbents Just Add This?

Net Worth Trackers

Would need to build:

  • SEBI RIA license (6-12 months)
  • 7-tier categorization engine
  • Surplus computation pipeline
  • Goal planning system
  • Investment analytics across MFs, NPS, gold, real estate
  • AI advisor with holistic financial data backend

Essentially our entire stack. 12+ month head start.

MF Distributors

Structural conflict:

  • Earn commissions from Regular MF plans
  • Fiduciary advice = "switch to Direct" = lose ₹47K/user/year in commissions
  • Distribution and advisory are legally incompatible
  • Would need separate RIA entity

Can't cannibalize their own revenue model.

Banks

  • Relationship managers push bank's own products
  • Cross-institutional view against their interest
  • Regulatory silos prevent full data aggregation
  • Innovation speed of legacy institutions

Generic AI Chatbots

  • No access to user's real financial data
  • No RIA license = can't recommend specific actions
  • Our advisor only makes claims derived from structured data — it cannot hallucinate your net worth or fund performance because those are computed values, not LLM outputs
What about a well-funded new startup? Our defense is the data flywheel + categorization corpus + first-mover RIA brand trust. A competitor can raise money and hire engineers — they can't buy 10,000 learned merchant patterns or 50 self-learning email templates. Network effects compound, code doesn't.
