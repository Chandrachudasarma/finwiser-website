Two 35-year-olds. Two answers.

Three pairs. Same age. Same payslip. Sometimes the same goal. Six different verdicts — because Finwy reads what the data says, not what the category suggests.

Riya and Aman both turned 35 last quarter. Both earn ₹98,000 take-home. Both call themselves disciplined investors. Then Finwy reads what they actually have.

Riya M. · 35 · Bengaluru

Software engineer

Opened Finwy after a colleague’s heart attack at 38.

Take-home
₹ 98,000 / mo
Surplus
₹ 52,000 / mo
Portfolio
₹ 40L MFs · ₹ 6L FD
Equity
83% of total
Term cover
₹ 50L
Self-rated
Conservative

  1. “My friends just added another equity SIP. Am I being too cautious or are they being reckless?”

  2. Riya thinks more equity is always better.

  3. Your friends are not your portfolio. You’re already 83% in equity at 35.

  4. Real gap: term at ₹50L is half what you need. Liquid buffer is 3 months — should be 9. Add ₹15K to liquid, not equity.

▰ Verdict — Stop adding equity. Buy term. Build liquidity.

If she doesn’t act: ₹1.5cr term gap. Every uncovered month is a coin flip on her family.

In 5 years on this path: ₹2cr term in place. 9-month buffer. Retirement on track.

  1. “Crypto. Gold. Smallcase. Everyone has an opinion. I have ₹6L in FD and a sinking feeling I’m behind.”

  2. Aman thinks he needs an exotic bet to catch up.

  3. Neither, yet. You have zero equity at 35 — that’s the bigger problem, not which flavour first.

  4. ₹25K/month index SIP, today. Crypto can wait two years. Gold can wait forever.

Risk appetite is not what you say. It’s what your data can absorb. Riya can’t take more equity risk. Aman is taking too little.

  1. “Should I increase my parents’ health cover?”

  2. Vivek thinks he should be doing more, more places.

  3. Your parents have pensions and routine health. Top up the family floater to ₹25L for ₹4,800/year. Done.

  4. Your real gap is elsewhere: emergency fund is 4 months, needs 6. Add ₹15K/mo to liquid for 8 months. Retirement is on track.

  2. Karan thinks the payslip is the whole story.

  3. Your real surplus is ₹30K, not ₹65K. Dialysis is ₹35K every month, every month.

  4. Emergency fund of ₹3L is 1.5 months of real outflow. Build ₹6L buffer first. Then term cover up to ₹2.5cr (only ₹6,500/year more).

▰ Verdict — Family first. Retirement second. Order matters.

In 5 years on this path: ₹6L buffer, ₹2.5cr term, retirement back on the table at 60.

Real surplus is what’s left after the obligations a payslip can’t see. Vivek’s payslip is his real surplus. Karan’s payslip is fiction.
